# Content And Design

## Subject, Preview, And Sender Identity

- Keep subject lines short, specific, and non-deceptive.
- Use preview text to complement, not repeat, the subject line.
- Keep sender identity consistent at team and brand level.
- Set reply-to behavior intentionally for the workflow.

## Nudgen-Oriented Content Notes

- Brand-aware sender identity should match selected team/brand configuration.
- Template and signature choices should be consistent with campaign goal.
- AI-assisted copy should be reviewed for claim accuracy and tone control.

## Layout And Accessibility

- Use clear hierarchy, concise copy, and one primary CTA.
- Keep email width and spacing readable on both desktop and mobile.
- Preserve contrast and legibility in dark mode.
- Avoid image-only messaging for critical content.

## Personalization

- Use personalization only from reliable fields.
- Define safe fallback values for missing personalization keys.
- Avoid over-personalization that feels synthetic or intrusive.

## Practical Review Checklist

- Subject and preview are aligned with body and CTA.
- All links and tracked links resolve correctly.
- Sender name, from, and reply-to are intentional.
- Signature and footer are present and policy compliant.
